# New Jersey Tax Guide

State-specific tax rules, deductions, and credits for NJ filers.

---

## NJ Income Tax Rates (2025)

NJ uses a graduated rate structure. Key brackets:

| Taxable Income (Single/MFS) | Taxable Income (MFJ/HoH) | Rate |
|-----------------------------|---------------------------|------|
| $0 – $20,000 | $0 – $20,000 | 1.4% |
| $20,001 – $35,000 | $20,001 – $50,000 | 1.75% |
| $35,001 – $40,000 | $50,001 – $70,000 | 3.5% |
| $40,001 – $75,000 | $70,001 – $80,000 | 5.525% |
| $75,001 – $500,000 | $80,001 – $150,000 | 6.37% |
| $500,001 – $1,000,000 | $150,001 – $500,000 | 8.97% |
| Over $1,000,000 | $500,001 – $1,000,000 | 10.75% |
| | Over $1,000,000 | 10.75% |

**Verify:** Search "NJ income tax rates {tax_year}" at https://www.state.nj.us/treasury/taxation/

## NJ Property Tax Deduction

NJ allows a deduction for property taxes paid on your **primary residence**.

- **Maximum deduction:** $15,000
- **Available to:** Homeowners and tenants (tenants deduct 18% of rent paid as property tax equivalent)
- **Form:** NJ-1040 Line 38a

This is one of the most valuable NJ-specific deductions. Unlike the federal SALT cap
($10,000), NJ has its own property tax deduction with a higher limit.

**How to ask:** "What was your total property tax on your primary residence in NJ?
Check your annual property tax bill — it's usually due in 4 quarterly installments."

---

## NJ Homestead Benefit

A property tax credit program for eligible NJ homeowners.

- **Eligibility:** NJ homeowner, income ≤ $150,000 (seniors 65+) or ≤ $75,000 (under 65)
- **Benefit:** Applied directly as a credit on your property tax bill
- **Application:** Filed separately (usually October–November), not part of the income tax return
- **Important:** Must file NJ income tax return to be eligible

**How to ask:** "Did you apply for the NJ Homestead Benefit? It's a separate application
from your tax return — NJ sends a notice in the fall."

## NJ Estimated Tax Payments

| Quarter | Due Date |
|---------|----------|
| Q1 | April 15 |
| Q2 | June 15 |
| Q3 | September 15 |
| Q4 | January 15 |

NJ estimated payments are made using Form NJ-1040-ES. Payments can be made online at
https://www.state.nj.us/treasury/taxation/payments.shtml

**Safe harbor:** Same as federal — pay 100% of prior year tax or 80% of current year tax.

## NJ-Specific Deductions and Exemptions

### Personal Exemptions
NJ still has personal exemptions (unlike federal):
- $1,000 per taxpayer
- $1,500 per dependent
- Additional $1,000 if 65+ or blind

### Medical Expenses
NJ allows a deduction for medical expenses exceeding 2% of NJ gross income
(more generous than the federal 7.5% AGI threshold).

### Retirement Income Exclusion
NJ excludes a portion of retirement income (pensions, annuities, IRA withdrawals) for residents 62+ with income ≤ $150,000 MFJ:
- Up to $100,000 exclusion for MFJ (if total income ≤ $150,000)
- Up to $75,000 for single
- Phased: only available if NJ gross income ≤ threshold

### NJ Earned Income Tax Credit
NJ has its own EITC equal to 40% of the federal EITC.

### NJ Child Tax Credit (new for 2025)
- **Credit:** Up to $1,000 per dependent age 5 or younger
- **Income limit:** Taxable income of $80,000 or less
- **Not available** if Married Filing Separately

**How to ask:** "Do you have children age 5 or under? NJ now offers a child tax credit
of up to $1,000 per qualifying child."

## NJ and Federal Differences

| Item | Federal | NJ |
|------|---------|-----|
| SALT deduction cap | $40,000 | No cap (property tax deduction up to $15,000) |
| Standard deduction | Yes | No — NJ uses personal exemptions |
| Medical expense threshold | 7.5% AGI | 2% NJ gross income |
| 401(k) contributions | Pre-tax for federal | Taxable for NJ (NJ taxes 401k contributions) |
| Roth IRA distributions | Tax-free | Tax-free |
| Capital gains | Preferential rates | Taxed as ordinary income |
| Municipal bond interest | Tax-exempt (own state) | Tax-exempt for NJ bonds only |

**Important NJ quirk:** NJ taxes 401(k) and 403(b) contributions — your NJ wages on
your W-2 are higher than your federal wages. This means NJ taxes you on money you
contributed to retirement. When you withdraw in retirement, NJ does NOT tax the
portion already taxed (to avoid double taxation). Track your NJ cost basis in
retirement accounts.

## NJ Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTE)

NJ allows pass-through entities (partnerships, S-corps, LLCs) to elect to pay NJ
income tax at the entity level. Members receive a credit on their NJ return.

This is a workaround for the federal SALT deduction cap — the entity-level tax
is a business expense (not subject to SALT cap), and the member gets a credit.

**How to ask:** "Did any of your K-1 entities elect to pay NJ PTE tax? You should
see a credit on your NJ K-1."

## NJ Filing Requirements

- **Residents:** File NJ-1040 if NJ gross income exceeds the filing threshold ($10,000 single, $20,000 MFJ).
- **Non-residents with NJ income:** File NJ-1040NR.
- **Part-year residents:** File NJ-1040 for the resident period.
- **Due date:** April 15 (same as federal). Extension: October 15 with NJ-630.
