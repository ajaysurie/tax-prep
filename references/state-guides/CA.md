# California Tax Guide

State-specific tax rules, deductions, and credits for CA filers.

---

## CA Income Tax Rates (2025)

CA has 9 graduated brackets from 1% to 12.3%, plus a 1% Mental Health Services Tax (MHST) surcharge on taxable income over $1,000,000 (all filing statuses), bringing the effective top rate to 13.3%.

### Schedule X -- Single or Married/RDP Filing Separately

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|----------------|------|
| $0 -- $11,079 | 1.00% |
| $11,080 -- $26,264 | 2.00% |
| $26,265 -- $41,452 | 4.00% |
| $41,453 -- $57,542 | 6.00% |
| $57,543 -- $72,724 | 8.00% |
| $72,725 -- $371,479 | 9.30% |
| $371,480 -- $445,771 | 10.30% |
| $445,772 -- $742,953 | 11.30% |
| Over $742,953 | 12.30% |
| Over $1,000,000 | +1.00% MHST |

### Schedule Y -- Married/RDP Filing Jointly or Qualifying Surviving Spouse

## CA Estimated Tax Payments

| Quarter | Due Date |
|---------|----------|
| Q1 | April 15 |
| Q2 | June 15 |
| Q3 | September 15 |
| Q4 | January 15 |

CA estimated payments use **Form 540-ES**. Payments can be made online at
https://www.ftb.ca.gov/pay/

**Safe harbor:** Pay 100% of prior year tax (110% if CA AGI > $150,000 single or
$300,000 MFJ) or 90% of current year tax to avoid underpayment penalty.

**How to ask:** "Did you make any California estimated tax payments during the year?
Check for payments made via Web Pay or mailed with Form 540-ES vouchers."

## CA-Specific Deductions and Credits

### CA Standard Deduction
CA has its own standard deduction, much lower than federal:
- **Single / MFS:** $5,706
- **MFJ / HoH / Qualifying Surviving Spouse:** $11,412

Unlike federal, CA does not have an additional standard deduction for age 65+ or
blindness -- those are handled through CA personal exemption credits.

### Nonrefundable Renter's Credit
A flat credit for CA renters who do not own their residence:
- **Single:** $60 (CA AGI $52,421 or less)
- **MFJ / HoH:** $120 (CA AGI $104,842 or less)

**How to ask:** "Did you rent your primary residence in California? What was your
approximate California adjusted gross income?"

### California Earned Income Tax Credit (CalEITC)
CA has its own refundable EITC for low-income working individuals:
- **Maximum credit:** Up to $3,756 (2025)
- **Income limit:** Earned income up to $32,900
- **Eligibility:** Must be 18+ (or have a qualifying child), have a valid SSN or ITIN
- Available to ITIN filers (unlike federal EITC)

### Young Child Tax Credit (YCTC)
An additional refundable credit for CalEITC-eligible filers with a child under age 6:
- **Maximum credit:** Up to $1,189 per return (2025)
- Must qualify for CalEITC (with exception: no earned income required if net loss
  up to $35,640 for tax year 2025)

### College Access Tax Credit
CA has a credit for contributions to the College Access Tax Credit Fund:
- 50% of contribution amount
- Credit is claimed on Form 3592
- Subject to annual fund cap -- first-come, first-served

### 529 Plan Treatment
**Important:** CA does **NOT** conform to any federal 529 deduction. Contributions to
529 plans (including CA ScholarShare 529) are not deductible on the CA return.
However, qualified distributions remain tax-free at the state level.

**How to ask:** "Did you contribute to a 529 college savings plan? Note that CA does
not offer a state deduction for 529 contributions, unlike some other states."

## CA and Federal Differences

| Item | Federal | CA |
|------|---------|-----|
| Standard deduction | $15,750 (S) / $31,500 (MFJ) | $5,706 (S) / $11,412 (MFJ) |
| SALT deduction cap | $40,000 | N/A -- CA does not allow SALT deduction on state return |
| Capital gains | Preferential rates (0/15/20%) | **Taxed as ordinary income** (up to 13.3%) |
| 401(k) contributions | Pre-tax | Pre-tax (CA follows federal) |
| Social Security | Up to 85% taxable | **Fully exempt from CA tax** |
| Pension/retirement | Taxable | Taxable (no exclusion like NY) |
| Municipal bond interest | Tax-exempt (own state) | Tax-exempt for CA bonds only |
| 529 contributions | No federal deduction | **No CA deduction** |
| Mortgage interest | Deductible (up to $750K) | Deductible (CA conforms to $750K limit for post-2017 loans) |

**Key CA quirk -- capital gains:** CA taxes all capital gains as ordinary income with
no preferential rate. A taxpayer in the top bracket pays 13.3% on long-term capital
gains (vs. 20% + 3.8% NIIT = 23.8% federal). For high earners with large capital
gains, the combined federal + CA rate can exceed 37%.

**How to ask:** "Did you have any capital gains this year? In California, these are
taxed at your ordinary income rate -- there is no preferential capital gains rate."

## CA Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTE)

CA allows qualifying pass-through entities (S-corps, partnerships, LLCs taxed as
partnerships) to elect to pay an entity-level state income tax as a SALT cap workaround.

- **Tax rate:** 9.3% of qualified net income
- **Election:** Made annually by the entity
- **Payment:** Form FTB 3893 -- first installment due June 15 of the election year,
  remainder due with the entity return
- **Member credit:** Qualified taxpayers claim a credit on their personal return using
  Form FTB 3804-CR
- **Extended through 2030** (originally set to expire after 2025)

The credit may exceed the member's CA tax liability and can generate a refund or
carry forward for up to 5 years.

**How to ask:** "Did any of your K-1 entities elect to pay CA PTE tax? You should see
a PTE credit on your CA K-1. Check for Form 3804-CR information."

**Verify:** https://www.ftb.ca.gov/file/business/credits/pass-through-entity-elective-tax/index.html

## CA-Specific Items

### CA State Disability Insurance (SDI)
- **2025 rate:** 1.2% of all wages (no wage cap since 2024 per SB 951)
- **Reported:** W-2 Box 14 as "CASDI" or "CA SDI"
- **Deductible:** On federal Schedule A as a state tax (subject to $40,000 SALT cap)
- SDI is NOT deductible on the CA return (you cannot deduct CA taxes on your CA return)

**How to ask:** "Check W-2 Box 14 for CA SDI withholding -- this is deductible on
your federal return as a state tax."

### CA LLC Fee (Gross Receipts Fee)
LLCs doing business in CA owe an annual fee based on total CA-source gross receipts,
in addition to the $800 minimum franchise tax:

| CA-Source Gross Receipts | Annual Fee |
|--------------------------|-----------|
| Less than $250,000 | $0 |
| $250,000 -- $499,999 | $900 |
| $500,000 -- $999,999 | $2,500 |
| $1,000,000 -- $4,999,999 | $6,000 |
| $5,000,000 and over | $11,790 |

Estimated fee payment (Form FTB 3536) is due by the 15th day of the 6th month
(June 15 for calendar-year LLCs).

### Behavioral Health Services Tax (formerly Mental Health Services Tax)
An additional 1% surcharge on CA taxable income exceeding $1,000,000 for all filing
statuses. This applies to both residents and nonresidents with CA-source income over
$1M. The threshold is NOT doubled for MFJ -- it is $1,000,000 regardless of filing
status. Effective top marginal rate: 12.3% + 1% = **13.3%**.

### Franchise Tax Board Minimum Tax
- **LLCs:** $800 annual minimum franchise tax (even if no income)
- **S-Corps:** $800 annual minimum franchise tax
- **C-Corps:** Minimum franchise tax of $800 or 8.84% of net income, whichever is greater
- First-year LLCs formed on or after January 1, 2024 are **exempt** from the $800
  minimum for their first tax year (per AB 85)

**How to ask:** "Do you have a California LLC or S-corp? There is an $800 annual
minimum franchise tax due to the FTB regardless of income."

## Multi-State Considerations

### CA Source Income Rules
CA taxes nonresidents on income sourced to California:
- **Wages/services:** Sourced based on where the work is physically performed
  (days worked in CA / total days worked worldwide)
- **Rental income:** Sourced to location of property
- **Business income:** Apportioned using CA's single sales factor formula
- **Capital gains from sale of CA real property:** CA-source income
- **Intangible income (stocks, etc.):** Generally sourced to state of residence,
  NOT to CA (for nonresidents)

### Remote Worker Rules
CA uses a **physical presence** test -- income is CA-source only to the extent
services are physically performed in California. Unlike NY, CA does NOT have a
"convenience of the employer" rule. If you work remotely from another state for a
CA employer, that income is generally NOT CA-source.

However, if you are a CA **resident** working remotely for an out-of-state employer,
CA taxes your worldwide income regardless of where you perform the work.

**How to ask:** "Do you live in one state but work for an employer in another state?
How many days did you physically work in California vs. outside California?"

### Credit for Taxes Paid to Other States
CA residents who pay income tax to another state on the same income can claim a
credit on Schedule S (Other State Tax Credit) to avoid double taxation. The credit
is limited to the lesser of the tax paid to the other state or the CA tax on that income.

## CA Filing Requirements

- **Residents:** File Form 540 if gross income exceeds the filing threshold
  (varies by filing status, age, and dependents -- generally $22,786 single under 65,
  $45,570 MFJ under 65 for 2025)
- **Nonresidents with CA income:** File Form 540NR
- **Part-year residents:** File Form 540NR with allocation schedule
- **Due date:** April 15. Extension: October 15 with Form FTB 3519
  (extension is automatic with payment -- no approval needed)
- **Extension payment:** If you owe, you must pay by April 15 even with an extension

**Verify:** https://www.ftb.ca.gov/file/personal/filing-situations/
