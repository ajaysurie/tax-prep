State-specific tax rules, deductions, and credits for NY filers.

### New York City (residents only)

NYC imposes an additional income tax on residents:

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---------------|------|
| $0 – $12,000 | 3.078% |
| $12,001 – $25,000 | 3.762% |
| $25,001 – $50,000 | 3.819% |
| Over $50,000 | 3.876% |

**NYC total effective rate = NYS rate + NYC rate.** This can push the combined
state+city rate above 14% for high earners.

### Yonkers
Yonkers residents pay a surcharge of 16.75% of their NY state tax.
Yonkers non-residents who work in Yonkers pay 0.5% of wages earned in Yonkers.

**Verify:** Search "NY income tax rates {tax_year}" at https://www.tax.ny.gov/

## Metropolitan Commuter Transportation Mobility Tax (MCTMT)

**What:** A payroll tax on self-employment income earned within the Metropolitan
Commuter Transportation District (MCTD) — NYC, Rockland, Nassau, Suffolk, Orange,
Putnam, Dutchess, Westchester counties.

**Who:** Self-employed individuals with net earnings from self-employment allocated
to the MCTD exceeding $50,000.

**Rate:** 0.34% of net self-employment earnings in the MCTD.

**How to ask:** "Do you have self-employment income earned in the NYC metro area?
If so, you may owe the Metropolitan Commuter Transportation Mobility Tax."

## NY-Specific Deductions and Credits

### 529 Plan Contributions
NY allows a deduction for contributions to a NY 529 plan:
- **Up to $5,000** per taxpayer ($10,000 MFJ)
- Must be a NY 529 plan (not out-of-state)
- Subject to recapture if withdrawn for non-qualified expenses within a certain period

**How to ask:** "Did you contribute to a New York 529 plan this year?"

### NY Standard Deduction
NY has its own standard deduction (different from federal):
- Single: $8,000
- MFJ: $16,050
- HoH: $11,200

### NY Itemized Deductions
NY itemized deductions start from federal itemized deductions but have limitations:
- NY AGI over $100,000: 25% reduction in certain itemized deductions
- NY AGI over $525,000: 50% reduction
- State/local income tax and real property tax deducted on the federal return
  must be added back for NY

### Child and Dependent Care Credit
NY has its own version of the federal credit:
- Available even if you claim the standard deduction
- Percentage based on NY AGI
- More generous than federal for lower-income filers

### NY Earned Income Credit
NY EIC = 30% of the federal EITC.
NYC residents get an additional NYC EIC = 5% of the federal EITC.

### College Tuition Credit/Deduction
NY allows either:
- A credit of up to $400 per student, OR
- A deduction of up to $10,000 per student
for undergraduate tuition at qualifying institutions.

## NY and Federal Differences

| Item | Federal | NY |
|------|---------|-----|
| Standard deduction | $15,750 (S) / $31,500 (MFJ) | $8,000 (S) / $16,050 (MFJ) |
| SALT deduction cap | $40,000 | No cap at state level (but addback rules apply) |
| Capital gains | Preferential rates (0/15/20%) | Taxed as ordinary income |
| 401(k) contributions | Pre-tax | Pre-tax (unlike NJ, NY follows federal) |
| Social Security | Up to 85% taxable | Fully exempt from NY tax |
| Pension/retirement | Taxable | First $20,000 exempt (age 59½+) |
| Municipal bond interest | Tax-exempt (own state) | Tax-exempt for NY bonds only |
| 529 contributions | No federal deduction | Up to $5,000/$10,000 deduction |

## NY Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET)

Similar to NJ, NY allows pass-through entities to elect to pay state tax at the
entity level. Members receive a credit on their NY return.

- The election is made annually by the entity
- Tax is paid on the entity's NY-source income
- Members claim the PTET credit on Form IT-653

**How to ask:** "Did any of your K-1 entities elect to pay NY PTET? Check your
NY K-1 for PTET credit information."

## Multi-State Considerations: NJ Resident Working in NY

This is a very common scenario. Key rules:

1. **NY taxes NY-source income** — wages earned in NY, rental income from NY property, etc.
2. **NJ taxes all worldwide income** as a resident.
3. **NJ gives credit for taxes paid to NY** on the same income (Form NJ-1040 Schedule A).
4. The credit is limited to the lesser of: NY tax on the income, or NJ tax on the income.
5. If NY tax rate > NJ tax rate on that income, you effectively pay the NY rate.

**For remote workers:** NY has a "convenience of the employer" rule — if you work
remotely from NJ for a NY employer but could have worked in NY, NY may still tax
that income as NY-source. This is controversial and under legal challenge.

**How to ask:** "Do you work in New York but live in New Jersey? Do you work remotely,
in-office, or hybrid?"

## NY Filing Requirements

- **Residents:** File IT-201 if NY AGI exceeds the filing threshold.
- **Non-residents with NY income:** File IT-203.
- **Part-year residents:** File IT-201 with allocation schedule.
- **NYC residents:** File NYC portion of IT-201 (no separate city return).
- **Due date:** April 15. Extension: October 15 with IT-370.
