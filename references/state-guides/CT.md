# Connecticut Tax Guide

State-specific tax rules, deductions, and credits for CT filers.

---

## CT Income Tax Rates (2025)

Connecticut has 7 graduated brackets from 2% to 6.99%. The two lowest rates were
reduced in 2024 (from 3% to 2% and 5% to 4.5%) — the largest CT income tax cut
in state history.

### Single or Married Filing Separately

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|----------------|------|
| $0 – $10,000 | 2% |
| $10,001 – $50,000 | 4.5% |
| $50,001 – $100,000 | 5.5% |
| $100,001 – $200,000 | 6% |
| $200,001 – $250,000 | 6.5% |
| $250,001 – $500,000 | 6.9% |
| Over $500,000 | 6.99% |

### Married Filing Jointly or Qualifying Surviving Spouse

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|----------------|------|
| $0 – $20,000 | 2% |
| $20,001 – $100,000 | 4.5% |
| $100,001 – $200,000 | 5.5% |
| $200,001 – $400,000 | 6% |
| $400,001 – $500,000 | 6.5% |
| $500,001 – $1,000,000 | 6.9% |
| Over $1,000,000 | 6.99% |

### Head of Household

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|----------------|------|
| $0 – $16,000 | 2% |
| $16,001 – $80,000 | 4.5% |
| $80,001 – $160,000 | 5.5% |
| $160,001 – $320,000 | 6% |
| $320,001 – $400,000 | 6.5% |
| $400,001 – $800,000 | 6.9% |
| Over $800,000 | 6.99% |

**Verify:** https://www.cga.ct.gov/2025/rpt/pdf/2025-R-0080.pdf and
https://portal.ct.gov/drs

## CT Personal Tax Credit

CT has a unique personal tax credit that reduces your tax liability by a percentage
based on CT AGI. This is NOT a deduction — it directly reduces tax owed.

| CT AGI (Single) | CT AGI (MFJ) | Credit % |
|-----------------|--------------|----------|
| ≤ $15,800 | ≤ $24,000 | 75% |
| $15,801 – $19,300 | $24,001 – $34,500 | 70% |
| $19,301 – $21,050 | $34,501 – $44,000 | 55% |
| $21,051 – $25,000 | $44,001 – $48,000 | 35% |
| $25,001 – $30,000 | $48,001 – $56,500 | 15% |
| Over $30,000 | Over $56,500 | 0% |

**Important:** The credit phases out completely at higher incomes. Most filers with
CT AGI over $56,500 (MFJ) receive no personal tax credit.

## CT-Specific Deductions and Credits

### Personal Exemptions

CT uses personal exemptions (not a standard deduction):
- **Single:** Up to $15,000
- **MFJ:** Up to $24,000
- **HoH:** Up to $19,000

Exemptions phase out at higher incomes:
- Single: phases out between $30,000 – $40,000 CT AGI
- MFJ: phases out between $48,000 – $64,000 CT AGI

### Property Tax Credit
- **Credit:** Up to $300 for CT property taxes paid on primary residence or motor vehicle
- **Income limits apply** — phases out at higher incomes
- Claim on Form CT-1040 Schedule 2

**How to ask:** "Did you pay property taxes on your Connecticut home or motor vehicle?
You may qualify for a property tax credit of up to $300."

### CT Earned Income Tax Credit
CT EITC is a **refundable** credit equal to **40%** of the federal EITC.
This is one of the most generous state EITCs in the country.

### CT Child Tax Credit
CT offers a refundable child tax credit of up to $250 per qualifying child
(under age 17). Income limits apply.

**How to ask:** "Do you have children under 17? Connecticut offers its own child
tax credit in addition to the federal credit."

### Pension and Annuity Exemption
CT exempts a portion of pension and annuity income for residents:
- Up to 100% exempt if CT AGI is below certain thresholds
- Phased in over several years (fully phased in by 2025)
- Social Security is also partially exempt from CT tax

### Social Security Exemption
CT has been phasing out its tax on Social Security benefits:
- For 2025, Social Security benefits are **fully exempt** for filers with
  CT AGI below $75,000 (single) or $100,000 (MFJ)
- Partial exemption above those thresholds

**How to ask:** "Do you receive Social Security benefits? Connecticut may exempt
some or all of your benefits depending on your income."

## CT and Federal Differences

| Item | Federal | CT |
|------|---------|-----|
| Standard deduction | $15,750 (S) / $31,500 (MFJ) | No standard deduction — uses personal exemptions |
| SALT deduction cap | $40,000 | N/A at state level |
| Capital gains | Preferential rates (0/15/20%) | Taxed as ordinary income (up to 6.99%) |
| 401(k) contributions | Pre-tax | Pre-tax (CT follows federal) |
| Social Security | Up to 85% taxable | Exempt below income thresholds |
| Municipal bond interest | Tax-exempt (own state) | Tax-exempt for CT bonds only |
| Property tax deduction | Itemized (subject to SALT cap) | Up to $300 credit |

## CT Pass-Through Entity Tax (PE Tax)

CT was one of the first states to implement a mandatory PTE tax as a SALT cap workaround.

- **Mandatory** for affected pass-through entities (not elective like other states)
- **Rate:** 6.99% on CT-source income
- **Member credit:** Members claim a credit on their CT return that offsets CT tax
- The credit may generate a refund

**How to ask:** "Did any of your K-1 entities pay CT pass-through entity tax? Check
your CT K-1 for PE Tax credit information."

## Multi-State Considerations

### CT Source Income Rules
- **Wages:** Sourced to where services are performed
- **Rental income:** Sourced to location of property
- **Business income:** Apportioned based on CT's allocation formula

### CT and NY Commuters
CT residents working in NY are taxed by both states. CT provides a credit for
taxes paid to other states on Form CT-1040 Schedule 2 to avoid double taxation.

**How to ask:** "Do you live in Connecticut but work in New York? You'll file in
both states, and CT will give you a credit for NY taxes paid."

## CT Filing Requirements

- **Residents:** File Form CT-1040 if CT AGI exceeds filing thresholds
  ($15,000 single, $24,000 MFJ)
- **Non-residents with CT income:** File Form CT-1040NR/PY
- **Part-year residents:** File Form CT-1040NR/PY
- **Due date:** April 15. Extension: October 15 with Form CT-1040 EXT
  (automatic 6-month extension with payment)
