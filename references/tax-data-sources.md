### SALT Deduction Cap

**$40,000** ($20,000 if Married Filing Separately) for 2025.
Covers state and local income tax (or sales tax) + property tax combined.

Raised from $10,000 to $40,000 by the One, Big, Beautiful Bill Act (P.L. 119-21).
Increases to $40,400 in 2026, then +1%/year through 2029. Reverts to $10,000 in 2030.

**Verify:** https://www.irs.gov/newsroom/one-big-beautiful-bill-provisions

**Safe harbor rules:**
- Pay ≥ 90% of current year tax, OR
- Pay ≥ 110% of prior year tax (100% if AGI ≤ $150,000 MFJ)
- Underpayment penalty rate: ~8% annualized (check IRS for current rate)

**Verify:** Search "IRS 2025 estimated tax payment due dates."

## Annual Update Process

This file and the state guides must be updated each year before tax season. Here is
the research process:

### Step 1: Federal Updates (November–December)

The IRS publishes inflation adjustments via Revenue Procedure each November for the
following tax year. Check these authoritative sources **in order**:

1. **IRS Revenue Procedure** (primary source): https://www.irs.gov/newsroom/inflation-adjusted-tax-items-by-tax-year
2. **IRS Publication 17** (comprehensive reference): https://www.irs.gov/publications/p17
3. **IRS Publication 501** (standard deduction, dependents): https://www.irs.gov/publications/p501
4. **IRS Publication 505** (withholding, estimated tax): https://www.irs.gov/publications/p505
5. **IRS Publication 969** (HSA limits): https://www.irs.gov/publications/p969
6. **IRS Topic 409** (capital gains thresholds): https://www.irs.gov/taxtopics/tc409
7. **IRS Retirement Plan Limits**: https://www.irs.gov/retirement-plans/cola-increases-for-dollar-limitations-on-benefits-and-contributions

Also check for mid-year legislation changes (like the One, Big, Beautiful Bill Act of
2025) that override the standard inflation adjustments:
- https://www.irs.gov/newsroom/one-big-beautiful-bill-provisions

### Step 2: State Updates (January–February)

Each state publishes updated rates and forms. Check the **official state tax authority
website** — not third-party sites:

| State | Authority | URL |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| NJ | Division of Taxation | https://www.nj.gov/treasury/taxation/ |
| NY | Dept. of Taxation and Finance | https://www.tax.ny.gov/ |
| CA | Franchise Tax Board (FTB) | https://www.ftb.ca.gov/ |
| CT | Dept. of Revenue Services (DRS) | https://portal.ct.gov/drs |

For each state, verify:
- [ ] Income tax brackets and rates
- [ ] Standard deduction / personal exemption amounts
- [ ] State-specific credits (EITC, property tax, child tax)
- [ ] Estimated payment due dates
- [ ] Pass-through entity tax rules
- [ ] Any new legislation or changes for the tax year

### Step 3: Cross-Reference

After updating, verify federal numbers referenced in state guides match. The comparison
tables in each state guide (e.g., "NJ and Federal Differences") must reflect current
federal numbers (standard deduction, SALT cap, etc.).

### Step 4: Update Schema Version

If the structure of any JSON schema changes, increment `schema_version` in
`references/schemas.md`.
