# S-Corp Home Office Deduction

S-Corp shareholders who work from home CANNOT claim a home office deduction on their personal return the way sole proprietors do. The deduction flows through the S-Corp.

---

## Why It's Different

- **Sole proprietors (Schedule C):** Deduct home office directly on Form 8829 or simplified method
- **S-Corp shareholders:** You are an employee of your S-Corp. Employees lost the home office deduction after the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (2017). Instead, the S-Corp reimburses you.

---

## The Accountable Plan Method

1. **S-Corp adopts an accountable plan** (a written policy allowing expense reimbursement)
2. **Shareholder calculates home office expense** using either:
   - Simplified: $5/sqft x office sqft (max 300 sqft = $1,500)
   - Regular: Actual home expenses x business-use percentage
3. **S-Corp reimburses the shareholder** — this is a business expense to the S-Corp
4. **Reimbursement is NOT taxable income** to the shareholder (not on W-2)
5. **S-Corp deducts it** as an ordinary business expense

---

## What to Collect

- Total home square footage
- Dedicated office square footage
- Home expenses: mortgage interest or rent, utilities, insurance, repairs
- Percentage calculation: office sqft / total sqft

---

## Common Mistakes

- Taking the deduction on personal return — not allowed for S-Corp shareholders
- Forgetting to set up the accountable plan — reimbursement becomes taxable
- Not documenting the exclusive-use requirement — must be dedicated space

---

## CPA Note

Flag for the CPA: "S-Corp shareholder uses home office. Accountable plan reimbursement should be processed through the S-Corp, not on the personal return."
